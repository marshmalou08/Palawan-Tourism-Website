About Palawan

The last ecological frontier of the Philippines.

The Last Frontier

Palawan is a long, narrow island province stretching 450 kilometres through the southwestern Philippines, bounded by the South China Sea and the Sulu Sea. It is the largest province in the country by land area and one of the most ecologically diverse places on Earth.

Palawan's extraordinary natural wealth has earned it the title of the Last Ecological Frontier of the Philippines — a living testament to what the archipelago once looked like before centuries of human settlement transformed its landscapes. The province encompasses ancient rainforests, limestone karst formations, sprawling mangrove systems, and coral reefs of global importance.

The capital, Puerto Princesa, sits on the eastern coast and serves as the primary gateway for travellers. From here, routes spread outward to El Nido's dramatic seascapes in the north, the sunken warships and mountain lakes of Coron to the northeast, the tranquil fishing village of Port Barton along the western coast, and the impossibly long white sand shore of San Vicente.

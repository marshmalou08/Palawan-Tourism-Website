Kayaking

Paddle Your Own Path

Kayaking is one of the most intimate ways to experience Palawan's coastline. Moving silently through mangrove channels at water level, paddling into sea caves accessible only by kayak, or gliding across the mirror-still surface of a lagoon at dawn — these are experiences that reward the traveller willing to move at a slower pace than a motorised boat allows.

In El Nido, kayaking through the Big and Small Lagoons is one of the most iconic activities in the Philippines. The entrance to the Small Lagoon requires paddling through a low gap in the limestone cliff — an immediate and dramatic transition from open sea to a hidden world of turquoise water and soaring karst walls. Further north, sea kayaking expeditions along the coast of Palawan's northern cape take multi-day routes through wilderness beaches and sea caves with overnight camping on uninhabited islands.

In Port Barton, kayaking through the mangrove river that flows into the bay is a serene experience, with kingfishers, monitors, and herons visible along the banks. In Sabang, near the Underground River, guided kayak tours take visitors through mangrove forest before the overland trail to the river mouth.

Palawan Gastronomy

Flavours of the Last Frontier

Palawan's cuisine is shaped by the sea, the forest, and the many cultures that have passed through its shores — from indigenous communities to Chinese traders, Spanish missionaries, and Filipino settlers from every region of the archipelago.

Must-Try Dishes

Tamilok

Palawan's most famous delicacy — woodworm harvested from rotting mangrove wood, eaten raw with vinegar, chilli, and onion. A must-try for adventurous eaters visiting Puerto Princesa.

Kinilaw na Isda

Freshly caught fish cured in local vinegar, ginger, onion, and chilli. The coastal equivalent of ceviche, showcasing Palawan's exceptional seafood at its freshest.

Crocodile Sisig

Puerto Princesa's iconic dish — farmed crocodile meat prepared sisig-style with onions, chilli, and calamansi on a sizzling plate. The meat is tender and mild.

Fresh Seafood

Talangka, sugpo, lapu-lapu, and tanigue — the seafood of Palawan is incomparable. Best enjoyed grilled or steamed beside the sea as the sun goes down.

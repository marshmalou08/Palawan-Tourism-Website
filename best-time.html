Best Time to Visit

Palawan is beautiful year-round — knowing the seasons helps you plan.

Palawan's Seasons

Palawan has two main seasons: the dry season (November to May) and the wet season (June to October). The timing of your visit will shape which activities are available and how the landscape looks and feels.

Peak Season — Dec to Apr

The driest, calmest months with the clearest water and most reliable sea conditions. Ideal for all water activities including island hopping, diving, and kayaking. Book accommodation well in advance as this is the busiest period, particularly December–January and Easter.

Shoulder Season — Nov & May

Excellent conditions with fewer tourists and lower prices than peak months. November is a transition month — seas are mostly calm and the landscape is lush after the rains. May is hot but still relatively dry, with good diving visibility.

Green Season — Jun to Oct

The southwest monsoon (habagat) brings heavy rain and rough seas to Palawan's western coast. Some island-hopping tours are suspended due to waves. However, the east coast and inland destinations remain accessible, prices drop significantly, and the landscape is dramatically green and lush.

For Tubbataha Diving

Tubbataha Reef is only accessible during its specific open season — March to June — when sea conditions allow liveaboard vessels to make the 12-hour crossing from Puerto Princesa. Book a year in advance as permits are strictly limited.

Month by Month

  • November–JanuaryExcellent — cooler, calmer, festive
  • February–AprilPeak — best weather, busiest, most expensive
  • MayGood — hot, thinning crowds, clear water
  • June–OctoberRainy — avoid west coast, east coast fine
  • March–JuneTubbataha liveaboard season

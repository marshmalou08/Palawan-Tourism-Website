Taytay

Historic fort, hidden lagoons, and island-studded bay.

Taytay — meaning "bridge" in Tagalog — was the first capital of Palawan under Spanish rule. Today it is a peaceful town on the northeastern coast, dominated by the imposing ruins of Fort Santa Isabel (1667) and surrounded by a bay dotted with limestone islands and hidden lagoons that rival El Nido's in beauty but see only a fraction of the visitors.

Highlights of Taytay

Fort Santa Isabel One of the oldest Spanish forts in western Palawan
Manguao Lake Birdwatcher's Eden
Kuyawyaw Falls Series of freshwater falls cascading through jungle

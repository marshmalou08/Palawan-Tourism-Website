Safety Tips

Travel Safely in Palawan

Palawan is among the safest tourist destinations in the Philippines. Common-sense precautions and respect for the natural environment will ensure a safe and enjoyable visit.

Water Safety

Always wear a life jacket on banca boats — these are provided on all accredited island-hopping tours. Check weather forecasts before departing; strong winds (amihan and habagat) can make sea conditions dangerous quickly. Never swim alone in open water. Respect depth and current warnings from your guide.

Marine Safety

Never stand on or touch coral — it is both harmful to the reef and dangerous to you. Be aware of jellyfish, particularly during certain seasons. Fire coral causes painful burns on contact. Lionfish and stonefish are present in some areas — avoid touching anything on the seafloor.

Health Precautions

Drink bottled or filtered water only. Use insect repellent at dusk and dawn, particularly in areas near standing water or mangroves. Dengue fever is present in the Philippines — long sleeves and repellent are your best protection. Consult a travel doctor for malaria prevention if visiting remote interior areas.

Sun and Heat

The tropical sun is intense even on cloudy days. Apply sunscreen every two hours, wear a hat and UV-protective clothing on the water, and stay hydrated. Heat exhaustion is a real risk during peak-season midday activities — rest during the hottest hours (12–3pm) where possible.

Personal Security

Palawan is generally safe for travellers. Use accredited tour operators and accommodation. Keep copies of your passport and travel documents. Petty theft can occur in crowded areas — keep valuables secured. Leave expensive jewellery at home or in a hotel safe.

Environmental Responsibility

Use only reef-safe sunscreen. Carry your rubbish off the islands — there is no waste collection on most uninhabited islands. Do not collect shells, coral, or marine life. Follow your guide's instructions in marine reserves. Support the environment that makes Palawan extraordinary.

How to Get There

Getting to Palawan

Palawan is accessible by air and sea from Manila and several other Philippine cities. Most international visitors fly to Manila first, then connect onward.

By Air to Puerto Princesa

Puerto Princesa International Airport (PPS) is the main gateway. Daily flights from Manila take approximately 1 hour 20 minutes. Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and AirAsia serve this route. Flights from Cebu, Clark, and Iloilo are also available seasonally.

By Air to El Nido

AirSWIFT operates daily flights from Manila to Lio Airport in El Nido (approximately 1 hour 20 minutes). This is the most convenient option for travellers heading directly to El Nido and bypassing Puerto Princesa.

By Air to Coron

Francisco B. Reyes Airport (Busuanga) serves Coron with daily flights from Manila operated by Cebu Pacific and Philippine Airlines. Flight time is approximately 1 hour 10 minutes.

By Ferry

2GO Travel and other ferry operators serve Puerto Princesa from Manila (approximately 2 days by sea). Ferries are a budget option but significantly slower than flying. RORO services connect the island chain between Coron, El Nido, and Puerto Princesa seasonally.

Getting Around Palawan

From Puerto Princesa: vans to El Nido (5–6 hours), Port Barton (3–4 hours), and San Vicente (4–5 hours) operate daily from the terminal. Tricycles serve short urban routes. Motorbike rental is popular in smaller towns like El Nido and Port Barton.

Between Islands

Fast ferries connect El Nido and Coron (4–4.5 hours) via San Jose and Port Barton, passing through some of Palawan's most scenic waters. These services operate from October to May, weather permitting.

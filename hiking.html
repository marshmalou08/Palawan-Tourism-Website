Hiking

Trails Through the Last Frontier

Palawan's interior is a world of dense tropical rainforest, karst limestone peaks, and hidden waterfalls — a landscape that rewards those willing to leave the beach and venture upward. Hiking options range from short jungle walks to multi-day expeditions into the mountains of southern Palawan.

In El Nido, the Taraw Cliff Trek rises steeply from the town to a limestone peak offering panoramic views of the Bacuit Archipelago. It is a challenging 3-4 hour return hike through forest and over exposed rock, best tackled at sunrise. The reward — an unobstructed 360-degree view over dozens of limestone islands scattered across the bay — is genuinely extraordinary.

Near Puerto Princesa, the Irawan Eco-Tourism Park offers forest trails, swimming holes, and a canopy walk through secondary rainforest. The trail to Estrella Falls near San Vicente passes through agricultural land and community forest before arriving at a multi-tiered waterfall fed by the mountains above Long Beach.

For serious trekkers, the mountains of the Palawan highlands in the south offer multi-day expeditions through old-growth forest accompanied by local indigenous guides who know the trails and ecosystems intimately.

Hiking Tips

  • Best TimeEarly morning, November to April
  • FootwearSturdy trail shoes or sandals with grip
  • GuideRequired for most inland trails
  • WaterBring at least 2 litres per person
  • Must-DoTaraw Cliff Trek, El Nido

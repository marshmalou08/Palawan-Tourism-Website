Cultural Tours

Meet the People of Palawan

A visit to Palawan that touches only the surface of its natural beauty misses one of the province's greatest gifts — its people. Cultural tours provide a respectful and meaningful window into the lives of Palawan's indigenous communities, whose knowledge, art, and ways of living are as extraordinary as the landscape they inhabit.

In Puerto Princesa, the Batak community of Tanabag welcomes visitors for educational tours that demonstrate traditional forest practices, weaving techniques, and the use of medicinal plants. The tour is operated by the community itself, with all fees going directly to the families involved. Nearby, the Tagbanua community of Honda Bay offers guided tours of their fishing grounds and village, explaining their traditional marine tenure system that has protected the reefs of the bay for generations.

The Ugod Festival in Puerto Princesa, held each January, brings together indigenous and settler communities in a celebration of Palawan's cultural diversity featuring traditional music, dance, food, and craft. The Baragatan Festival in June is the province-wide celebration that showcases the cultures of all Palawan's municipalities with processions, performances, and the crowning of a festival queen.

All cultural tours should be arranged through accredited operators or directly with communities. Visitors are asked to dress modestly, ask permission before photographing individuals, and purchase crafts directly from the artisans who made them.

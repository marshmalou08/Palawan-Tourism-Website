Snorkeling & Diving

Into the Deep

Palawan sits within the Coral Triangle — the most biodiverse marine region on Earth — and its underwater world reflects this extraordinary richness. From shallow reef gardens accessible to first-time snorkellers to technical wreck dives at 40 metres, the province offers something for every level of underwater explorer.

In El Nido, the marine reserve protects a system of reefs, seagrass beds, and mangroves that shelter thousands of fish species, sea turtles, and the occasional whale shark. Snorkelling directly from island-hopping boats at sites like Shimizu Island and Miniloc Island reveals coral gardens of breathtaking colour and vitality.

Coron is the wreck diving capital of the Philippines. Twelve Japanese ships sunk by American aircraft in September 1944 lie on the seafloor of Coron Bay, now colonised by coral and inhabited by clouds of fish. The Okikawa Maru, Irako, and Akitsushima are among the finest wreck dives in Asia. Coron also offers lake diving in Barracuda Lake, a thermocline lake with eerie geological formations and extreme visibility.

Tubbataha Reef, accessible only by liveaboard from Puerto Princesa during the March-to-June window, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site where hammerhead sharks, manta rays, whale sharks, and sea turtles congregate in waters of exceptional clarity around two remote coral atolls in the Sulu Sea.

Key Dive Sites

  • El NidoShimizu Island, Dilumacad reef
  • CoronOkikawa Maru, Irako, Barracuda Lake
  • TubbatahaLiveaboard only, March–June
  • LevelBeginner to advanced/technical
  • CertificationPADI/SSI available in El Nido and Coron

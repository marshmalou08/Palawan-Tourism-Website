Packing Essentials

What to Pack for Palawan

Palawan is primarily a beach and outdoor destination. Travel light, bring what you need for the water and the jungle, and leave the rest at home.

Sun Protection

Reef-safe sunscreen is essential and mandatory in El Nido's marine reserve. Chemical sunscreens kill coral — bring mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide or titanium dioxide) only. UV-protective rash guards or swim shirts save you from both sunburn and the need for constant reapplication.

Clothing

Light, breathable clothing — linen and cotton are ideal in the tropical heat. Swimwear for water activities. A light rain jacket for afternoon showers. Modest clothing for cultural sites and indigenous community visits. Most restaurants are casual — smart-casual is sufficient for the finest venues.

Footwear

Water sandals or aqua shoes for island hopping over rocky shores and coral. Comfortable walking shoes or trail sandals for town exploration and light hikes. Flip-flops for the beach. Leave the heels at home.

Gear

Dry bag (essential — every island-hopping boat gets splashed). Snorkelling mask and fins if you have a preferred fit. Waterproof phone case. Headlamp for power outages in remote areas. Small first-aid kit with antiseptic cream for coral cuts.

Health

Insect repellent (DEET-based for evenings near mangroves and forests). Anti-malaria medication if visiting remote interior areas — consult a travel doctor. Rehydration sachets for hot days. Basic antihistamines for insect bites. Hand sanitiser for areas without running water.

Money & Documents

Sufficient cash in Philippine Pesos — ATMs are limited and sometimes out of service. Photocopies of passport stored separately from the original. Travel insurance documentation. Any required environmental fee vouchers (some areas sell these online in advance).
